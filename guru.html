GURU · 2026

See it. Say it.

An AI concierge woven into a live travel site. The content comes first; the conversation follows — and it acts on the page you are looking at.

CHAPTER 01

Content first, conversation second.

Waycraft starts from an empty chat: you describe a trip, and AI generates the content. Guru inverts the order — twenty years of curated China journeys are already on screen, and you simply react to what you see.

WAYCRAFT · CHAT-FIRST
Waycraft
say it → AI generates → see it

You start from a blank conversation. The AI listens to intent, then builds the itinerary, map and lodging from scratch.

GURU · CONTENT-FIRST
Guru
see it → say it → AI acts on it

You start from real journeys already on screen. The AI works on the page itself — highlighting, opening, recomposing what you see.

A blank prompt asks people to imagine; a composed page lets them recognise. Faced with real itineraries, real prices and real photography, nobody freezes — they point. Guru is built around that pointing.

CHAPTER 02

Say it where you see it.

Ask about Shanghai and the concierge doesn't paste links into a chat — it highlights the matching packages right on the page, then opens the one that catches your eye.

The model shares the page's live state — which city you are browsing, which package is open, which section is on screen. A fixed set of verbs, highlightPackages and openPackageDetails among them, lets it act on the page instead of describing it.

CHAPTER 03

One sentence, a recomposed journey.

“Shanghai Disney, plus five days in Hangzhou.” The concierge assembles a multi-day journey out of real packages — day by day, priced, mapped — without ever leaving the page.

Composition, not generation: every day in the plan is a real, bookable package from the catalogue. The AI arranges inventory — it doesn't invent it. One more tap and the whole journey lands on a route map.
